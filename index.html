Exam Techniques

  1. Spaced Repetition Intervals: Review your subject flashcards 1 day, 3 days, and then 7 days after learning them to push facts into long-term memory.
  2. Active Blurting Method: Read a syllabus section for 5 minutes, close the textbook, and write down everything you can recall in red ink to instantly spot knowledge gaps.
  3. Pomodoro Energy management: Study in focused blocks of 25 minutes, followed by a 5-minute break away from screens to prevent cognitive fatigue.
  4. Tackle Blind Spots Early: Sort your topics using a red, amber, green tracking system. Spend your sharpest morning energy fixing your "red" topics first.
  5. Simulate Untimed Past Papers: Work through real exam papers without a clock to get comfortable applying formulas and organizing paragraph links safely.
  6. Transition to Timed Conditions: Once confident, complete past papers using strict structural timers to train yourself not to run out of time on long-form marks.
  7. Create Structural Command Word Maps: Memorize exactly what examiners want when they use verbs like "Evaluate", "Explain", or "Compare".
  8. Protect Circadian Sleep Cycles: Aim for 8 hours of sleep before big tests. Your brain processes, organizes, and stores what you learned during deep sleep phases.
  9. Optimize Workspace Clarity: Keep your revision desk clean and leave your smartphone in another room to prevent your attention from breaking focus.
  10. Utilize Positive Reward Loops: Schedule guilt-free personal breaks, exercise runs, or gaming sessions directly after completing tough study milestones.
